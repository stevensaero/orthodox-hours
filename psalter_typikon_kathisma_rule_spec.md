# Psalter — Typicon Ch. 17 Kathisma Distribution / "Rule of the Holy Fathers" (Spec)

**Status:** Capture only. Not scheduled. Phase 2, follows the Service of the Psalter
(`psalter_departed_spec.md`).
**Source:** *A Psalter for Prayer* (Jordanville), pp. 28–32 — "A Rule of the Holy
Fathers," stated to be Typicon Ch. 17 verbatim.

---

## Purpose

This is the **date-driven complement** to the departed rite. The departed reading is
calendar-independent (continuous); **normal** Psalter reading is governed by the
Typicon: *which* kathismata are appointed at *which* service on *which* day, across the
seasonal regimes. The hours-tool already has the engine for this (`computePascha`,
Pascha-offset logic), so the value-add is: given a date, show **today's appointed
kathismata and the rubric why**, linking into the reader.

K = kathisma. "Hours" = 3rd/6th/9th Hours collectively unless noted.

---

## Regime 1 — Thomas Sunday → apodosis of the Exaltation (≈ Sep 21)
Light schedule (2 kathismata at Matins).

| Day | Matins | Vespers |
|-----|--------|---------|
| Sat | — (Vespers below) | K1 (Blessed is the man) |
| Sun | K2, K3 | — |
| Mon | K4, K5 | K6 |
| Tue | K7, K8 | K9 |
| Wed | K10, K11 | K12 |
| Thu | K13, K14 | K15 |
| Fri | K19, K20 | K18 |
| Sat | K16, K17 | (K1 — start of next cycle) |

- **K17 on Sundays:** omitted when the polyeleos is sung; on non-polyeleos Sundays, K17
  **is** said at Matins, with its troparia.
- **Sunday Vespers:** the Psalter is never read at Sunday Vespers all year — *except* if
  a feast occurs, when "Blessed is the man" (1st antiphon) is sung.
- **Midnight Office:** Mon–Fri → K17 (Blessed are the blameless); Sat → K9 (Unto Thee,
  O God, belongeth praise in Zion).
- **Feast on a Sunday:** after the two kathismata → polyeleos, megalynarion, then
  troparia "The assembly of angels" (no Glory / Both now / Alleluia), then the sessional
  hymn of the feast or saint.

## Regime 2 — after Exaltation apodosis (≈ Sep 22) → Dec 19
Heavier schedule (3 kathismata at Matins). Vespers all week: **K18**.

| Day | Matins |
|-----|--------|
| Sun | K2, K3 (+ polyeleos: Ps 134 & 135; troparia "The assembly of angels"; Hypakoe of the tone) |
| Mon | K4, K5, K6 |
| Tue | K7, K8, K9 |
| Wed | K10, K11, K12 |
| Thu | K13, K14, K15 |
| Fri | K19, K20 |
| Sat | K16, K17 |

- Sat Vespers: K1.
- **Feast on a 3-kathisma day:** first two at Matins, the 3rd at Vespers.
- **Feast on a Sunday:** after Ps 134/135 → megalynarion of the feast, the selected
  psalm, troparia "The assembly of angels," sessional hymn of the feast.
- The 3-kathisma rule begins from Sep 22; even if Sep 20/21 is a Sunday, the
  three-kathisma recitation begins from the Monday.

## Regime 4 — Jan 15 → Saturday before the Sunday of the Prodigal Son
3 kathismata at Matins; **K18** at Vespers (When I was in trouble I called upon the Lord).

---

## Regime 5 — Pre-Lent Sundays (Prodigal, Meatfare, Cheesefare)
- **Meatfare & Cheesefare Sundays:** 2 kathismata at Matins, the 3rd in sequence at
  Vespers ("because the brethren have little rest").
- **Prodigal, Meatfare, Cheesefare** at Matins: after Ps 134 & 135, add **Ps 136**
  (By the waters of Babylon) with the beautiful Alleluia, then troparia "The assembly of
  angels," and the Hypakoe of the tone.
- **Polyeleos season note:** from Cheesefare Sunday until the Exaltation, the polyeleos
  is sung only at vigils of feasts of the Lord. After the Exaltation apodosis until
  Cheesefare — except the fore/afterfeasts of Nativity and Theophany — the polyeleos is
  sung on Sundays at Matins in cathedrals, large churches, and larger monastic communities.

## Regime 8 — Holy Week (Lazarus Saturday → Great Wednesday)
Recited **only in church**.

| Day | Matins | Hours | Vespers |
|-----|--------|-------|---------|
| Lazarus Sat (Vespers) | — | — | K1 |
| Palm Sun | K2, K3 | — | — |
| Mon | K4, K5, K6 | K7, K8 (1st & 9th Hour: none) | K18 |
| Tue | K9, K10, K11 | K12, K13 (1st & 9th Hour: none) | K18 |
| Great Wed | K14, K15, K16 | K19, K20 (1st Hour: none) | K18 (without bows) |

After Great Wednesday Vespers the Psalter is **laid aside until the eve of Thomas
Sunday at Vespers** (i.e., no Psalter Thursday of Holy Week through Bright Week).

## Movable & fixed anchors → existing engine

Movable (from `computePascha` + Pascha offset):
- Thomas Sunday = Pascha + 7
- Prodigal Sunday = Pascha − 70; Meatfare Sunday = Pascha − 56; Cheesefare Sunday = Pascha − 49
- Great Lent weeks 1–6; Lazarus Saturday = Pascha − 8; Palm Sunday = Pascha − 7;
  Holy Week = Pascha − 6 … − 1

Fixed (plain dates, on the church calendar):
- Apodosis of the Exaltation ≈ Sep 21; the Dec 19/20 boundary; apodosis of Theophany ≈ Jan 14
